Projects

Medical Phantom

Worked in Computational Engineering and Robotics Lab at CMU and in cooperation with Alleghaney General Hospital to develop more realistic hand models for preoperative surgical planning and resident training. The model relied heavily on three-dimensional printing and other novel fabrication techniques.

Paradiddles For Days
UAV Computer Vision

Aided in developing software for a drone that eventually would allow it to detect cracks in large water tunnels. My research focused primarily on the development of a superpixel segmentation algorithm for the drone.

MouseTrap Car

I worked with a team of two other students to make a wooden car that could tavel forward 15 feet and backwards 15 feet only being powered by a mousetrap. After several design iterations, the car was made faster and more consistent.

C0 Virtual Machine

I implemented a virtual machine for an introductory version of C taught at CMU called c0. The virtual machine succesfully executes abritrary c0 code by taking care of the low-level memory and data manipulations that c0 requires to ensure safety.

Garage Band VR

Garage Band VR was a Hackathon Project I completed for SteelHacks. I used the Leap Motion VR controller to allow users to play various musical instruments in augumented reality.
